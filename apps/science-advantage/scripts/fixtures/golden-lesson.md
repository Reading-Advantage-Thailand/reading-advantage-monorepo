## Key Vocabulary

- **Photosynthesis** (Thai: การสังเคราะห์ด้วยแสง) - The process by which plants convert sunlight into energy
- **Chlorophyll** (Thai: คลอโรฟิลล์) - The green pigment in plants that captures sunlight
- **Glucose** (Thai: กลูโคส) - A type of sugar that plants produce during photosynthesis

## Reading Passage

Plants are amazing organisms that can create their own food through a process called photosynthesis. During this process, plants use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to produce glucose and oxygen.

The leaves of a plant contain tiny structures called chloroplasts, which contain chlorophyll. This green pigment absorbs sunlight and uses its energy to power the chemical reactions of photosynthesis.

## Materials

- 2 small potted plants
- One per student magnifying glass
- Several sheets of paper
- Science notebook and pencil

## Procedure

1. Examine your plants carefully using the magnifying glass
  - Look at the color and shape of the leaves
  - Count the number of leaves on each plant
2. Place one plant in direct sunlight
3. Place the other plant in a dark closet
4. Observe both plants daily for one week
  - Record your observations in your science notebook
  - Draw pictures of any changes you see

## Discussion Questions

What do you think will happen to each plant? Why do plants need sunlight to survive?
