# Thai National Science Curriculum Standards

## Grades 3-9 (Prathom 3 - Mattayom 3)

### Basic Education Core Curriculum B.E. 2551 (A.D. 2008)

---

## CURRICULUM OVERVIEW

### Structure

- **Primary Level (Grades 3-6)**: 80 hours per year
- **Lower Secondary Level (Grades 7-9)**: 120 hours per year (3 credits)
- **All 8 strands must be taught at every grade level**
- **Approach**: Learner-centered, inquiry-based, standards-based

### Eight Science Strands

**Strand 1: Living Things and Life Processes** (Sc1.1, Sc1.2)

- Understanding basic units, structures, functions, genetics, evolution, biodiversity, biotechnology

**Strand 2: Life and the Environment** (Sc2.1, Sc2.2)

- Understanding ecosystems, relationships, natural resource management

**Strand 3: Substances and Properties of Substances** (Sc3.1, Sc3.2)

- Understanding properties, structures, states of matter, solutions, chemical reactions

**Strand 4: Forces and Motion** (Sc4.1, Sc4.2)

- Understanding electromagnetic, gravitational, nuclear forces and motion types

**Strand 5: Energy** (Sc5.1)

- Understanding energy relationships, transformation, environmental effects

**Strand 6: Earth's Processes of Change** (Sc6.1)

- Understanding Earth's surface/interior processes, climate, topography changes

**Strand 7: Astronomy and Space** (Sc7.1, Sc7.2)

- Understanding solar system, galaxies, universe, space technology

**Strand 8: Nature of Science and Technology** (Sc8.1)

- Applying scientific process, reasoning, understanding science-technology-society connections

## GRADE 3 (PRATHOM 3)

**Annual Learning Time**: 80 hours

### Strand 1: Living Things & Life Processes

**Core Topics:**

- General characteristics of living things
- Diversity of living things
- Basic classification of organisms
- Simple life processes (growth, reproduction)

**Learning Indicators:**

- Identify and describe characteristics that distinguish living from non-living things
- Observe and record basic life processes
- Compare similarities and differences among living things
- Use simple investigative processes to study living things

### Strand 2: Life & the Environment

**Core Topics:**

- Basic environmental relationships
- Simple food chains
- Habitats and organism needs
- Basic conservation concepts

**Learning Indicators:**

- Describe relationships between organisms and their environment
- Create simple food chains showing energy flow
- Explain how organisms depend on their environment

### Strand 3: Substances & Properties

**Core Topics:**

- Observable properties of substances (color, shape, texture, odor)

**Learning Indicators:**

- Observe and describe properties of common substances

- Identify the three states of matter in everyday objects
- Investigate how properties determine uses

**Core Topics:**

- Push and pull forces
- Simple motion (fast/slow, direction)
- Effects of forces on objects
- Gravity in daily life

**Learning Indicators:**

- Demonstrate push and pull forces

### Strand 5: Energy

**Core Topics:**

- Basic forms of energy (light, heat, sound)
- Energy sources (sun, batteries, food)

**Learning Indicators:**

- Identify different forms of energy in daily life

### Strand 6: Earth's Processes

**Core Topics:**

- Basic characteristics of Earth's surface (land, water)
- Simple weather patterns
- Day and night cycle
- Seasons

**Learning Indicators:**

- Describe major features of Earth's surface

### Strand 7: Astronomy & Space

**Core Topics:**

- Sun, Earth, and Moon relationships
- Day and night

**Core Topics:**

## ASSESSMENT FRAMEWORK FOR GRADES 8-9

### Types of Assessment

**Formative Assessment (Ongoing):**

- Daily observations and questioning
- Lab notebook checks
- Quick quizzes and exit tickets
- Peer and self-assessment
- Concept mapping and graphic organizers

**Summative Assessment (End of Unit/Semester):**

- Unit tests (multiple choice, short answer, problem-solving)
- Laboratory practical exams
- Research projects and presentations
- Written lab reports
- Portfolio assessments

### Assessment Criteria

Students must demonstrate:

1. **Knowledge** - Understanding of scientific concepts and principles
2. **Application** - Ability to apply knowledge to new situations
3. **Investigation** - Use of scientific process and inquiry skills
4. **Communication** - Clear expression of scientific ideas
5. **Critical Thinking** - Analysis, evaluation, and reasoning

### Graduation Requirements for Grade 9

Students must:

- Achieve passing grades in all 8 strands
- Complete required lab work and investigations
- Demonstrate scientific literacy and reasoning
- Show understanding of science-technology-society connections
- Pass national science assessments
