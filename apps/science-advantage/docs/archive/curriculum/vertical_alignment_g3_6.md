# Science Curriculum Vertical Alignment: Grades 3-6

This document outlines the vertical progression of key science concepts from Grade 3 to Grade 6, aligned with the Thai National Standards. The curriculum is designed to build upon prior knowledge, introducing concepts at an introductory level and revisiting them with increasing depth and complexity in subsequent grades.

---

## Strand 1: Living Things & Life Processes

| Grade | Unit Title                              | Key Concepts                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| :---- | :-------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **3** | Introduction to Science & Living Things | - Characteristics of living things (7 life processes)<br>- Diversity of living things (plants, animals)<br>- Basic life processes (growth, reproduction)<br>- Basic needs of plants and animals                                                                                                                                                               |
| **4** | Body Systems & Structures               | - Introduction to major human organ systems (Digestive, Circulatory, Respiratory)<br>- How systems work together<br>- Structures and functions of plants (roots, stems, leaves) and animals (adaptations)<br>- Life cycles of various organisms (plants, insects, amphibians)                                                                                 |
| **5** | Human Body Systems                      | - Detailed structure and function of organ systems (Nervous system added)<br>- Homeostasis: maintaining internal balance<br>- Introduction to cells as the basic unit of life<br>- Plant vs. animal cell structures and functions<br>- Levels of biological organization (cells to organisms)<br>- Plant and animal reproduction (pollination, fertilization) |
| **6** | Integrated Body Systems & Genetics      | - Advanced system integration and feedback mechanisms (homeostasis in depth)<br>- Introduction to genetics: heredity, dominant/recessive traits, genes, DNA<br>- Detailed cellular processes: photosynthesis and respiration<br>- Cell division (mitosis)<br>- Introduction to microorganisms (bacteria, viruses)                                             |

## Strand 2: Life & the Environment

| Grade | Unit Title                       | Key Concepts                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| :---- | :------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **3** | Environments & Habitats          | - Basic needs of organisms<br>- Different types of habitats<br>- Simple food chains and introduction to food webs<br>- Introduction to conservation                                                                                                                              |
| **4** | Ecosystems & Relationships       | - Components of an ecosystem (biotic, abiotic)<br>- Roles in an ecosystem (producers, consumers, decomposers)<br>- Building food webs and understanding energy flow (energy pyramids)<br>- Human impact on ecosystems (pollution, habitat destruction)                           |
| **5** | Ecosystems & Biodiversity        | - Analysis of complex food webs and keystone species<br>- Nutrient cycles (Carbon, Nitrogen, Water)<br>- Natural resource management (renewable vs. non-renewable)<br>- Threats to biodiversity                                                                                  |
| **6** | Ecosystem Balance & Conservation | - Ecosystem balance: limiting factors, carrying capacity<br>- Population dynamics (predator-prey relationships)<br>- Levels of biodiversity (species, genetic, ecosystem)<br>- Advanced conservation strategies and sustainable resource management<br>- Extinction and recovery |

## Strand 3: Substances & Properties

| Grade | Unit Title                     | Key Concepts                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| :---- | :----------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **3** | Matter & Materials             | - Observable properties of materials (color, texture, hardness)<br>- Classifying materials (wood, metal, plastic)<br>- Introduction to states of matter (solid, liquid, gas)<br>- How material properties determine their use                   |
| **4** | Mixtures & Solutions           | - Physical vs. chemical properties<br>- Pure substances vs. mixtures<br>- Introduction to solutions (solute, solvent)<br>- Factors affecting dissolving<br>- Basic separation techniques (filtration, evaporation, magnetism)                   |
| **5** | Chemical Changes & Reactions   | - Identifying signs of chemical change<br>- Factors affecting reaction rates (temperature, concentration)<br>- Introduction to acids and bases and the pH scale<br>- Reversible vs. irreversible changes                                        |
| **6** | States of Matter & Separations | - Phase changes (melting, freezing, evaporation, etc.) and energy<br>- Particle theory to explain states of matter<br>- Types of mixtures (solutions, suspensions, colloids)<br>- Advanced separation techniques (distillation, chromatography) |

---

## Strand 4: Forces & Motion

| Grade | Unit Title              | Key Concepts                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| :---- | :---------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **3** | Forces & Motion         | - Forces as pushes and pulls<br>- How forces affect motion (start, stop, change direction)<br>- Describing motion (fast/slow, direction)<br>- Introduction to gravity and friction                                                                     |
| **4** | Forces in Action        | - Types of forces (tension, compression, torsion)<br>- Introduction to pressure<br>- Buoyancy (floating and sinking) and density<br>- Useful vs. unwanted friction and methods for reduction                                                           |
| **5** | Complex Forces & Motion | - Balanced vs. unbalanced forces and net force<br>- Speed, velocity, and acceleration<br>- Graphing motion (distance-time graphs)<br>- Introduction to simple machines (levers, pulleys, etc.)<br>- Introduction to work in physics (force x distance) |
| **6** | Complex Force Systems   | - Analysis of multiple forces (vector addition)<br>- Application of Newton's Laws of Motion<br>- Momentum (conservation and collisions)<br>- Forces in structures (stability, center of gravity)<br>- Pressure in fluids (Pascal's principle)          |

---

## Strand 5: Energy

| Grade | Unit Title                       | Key Concepts                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| :---- | :------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **3** | Energy All Around Us             | - Introduction to energy as the ability to cause change<br>- Basic forms of energy (light, heat, sound)<br>- Basic energy sources (sun, food, batteries)<br>- Simple energy transformations (e.g., battery to light)                                                                         |
| **4** | Energy Transformations           | - Introduction to mechanical and electrical energy<br>- Properties of light (reflection, shadow formation)<br>- Introduction to simple electrical circuits (series vs. parallel)<br>- Tracing energy transformations in simple devices                                                       |
| **5** | Energy Systems                   | - Complex, multi-step energy transformations<br>- Law of Conservation of Energy<br>- Heat transfer mechanisms (conduction, convection, radiation)<br>- Renewable and non-renewable energy sources<br>- Introduction to energy efficiency                                                     |
| **6** | Energy Conservation & Efficiency | - Detailed analysis of energy systems and efficiency calculations<br>- Electrical circuits in detail (Voltage, Current, Resistance - Ohm's Law)<br>- Electrical power and energy calculations (kWh)<br>- Energy use and its connection to climate change<br>- Energy conservation strategies |

## Strand 6: Earth's Processes

| Grade | Unit Title               | Key Concepts                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| :---- | :----------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **3** | Our Changing Earth       | - Earth's surface features (landforms, bodies of water)<br>- Introduction to rocks and soil<br>- Weather and seasons (focus on Thailand)<br>- The day/night cycle                                                                            |
| **4** | Earth's Dynamic Surface  | - Earth's surface components (crust, soil, water, air)<br>- The atmosphere and its importance<br>- Weather vs. climate<br>- The water cycle (evaporation, condensation, precipitation)                                                       |
| **5** | Earth's Changing Surface | - Weathering (physical and chemical) and erosion<br>- Soil formation, layers (horizons), and types<br>- The three types of rocks (igneous, sedimentary, metamorphic)<br>- Introduction to the rock cycle                                     |
| **6** | Earth's Cycles & Climate | - The rock cycle in detail<br>- The water cycle's role in global climate<br>- The carbon cycle and its connection to climate change<br>- Introduction to plate tectonics, earthquakes, and volcanoes<br>- Natural disasters and preparedness |

## Strand 7: Astronomy & Space

| Grade | Unit Title               | Key Concepts                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| :---- | :----------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **3** | The Sky Above            | - The Sun as a star that provides light and heat<br>- Earth's rotation causing day and night<br>- The Moon and its phases<br>- Introduction to stars and constellations                                                                         |
| **4** | Solar System Exploration | - The Sun-Earth-Moon system in more detail<br>- The cause of tides (Moon's gravity)<br>- The cause of seasons (Earth's tilt)<br>- Eclipses (solar and lunar)<br>- Introduction to the planets of the solar system                               |
| **5** | The Solar System         | - Formation of the solar system<br>- Detailed look at inner and outer planets<br>- Small solar system bodies (asteroids, comets)<br>- Gravity and orbits<br>- History of space exploration and its technology                                   |
| **6** | Solar System & Universe  | - Comparative planetology (analyzing and comparing planets)<br>- Moons and ring systems<br>- Universal gravitation (Newton's Law) and orbital mechanics (Kepler's Laws)<br>- Introduction to stars, galaxies, and the structure of the universe |

## Strand 8: Nature of Science & Technology

| Grade | Unit Title                            | Key Concepts                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| :---- | :------------------------------------ | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **3** | Integrated Science Applications       | - The scientific process (observe, question, predict, test, conclude)<br>- Basic science tools and safety<br>- Recording and communicating observations<br>- Introduction to technology as helpful tools                                                                |
| **4** | Science Process & Technology          | - Introduction to variables (independent, dependent, controlled)<br>- Designing fair tests<br>- Organizing and graphing data<br>- Drawing conclusions from evidence<br>- Positive and negative impacts of technology                                                    |
| **5** | Scientific Investigation & Technology | - Designing more complex experiments with multiple variables<br>- Basic statistical analysis (mean, median, mode)<br>- Advanced graphing and interpretation<br>- Evaluating technology using multiple criteria<br>- Introduction to ethics in science                   |
| **6** | Advanced Scientific Thinking          | - Logic in science (inductive vs. deductive reasoning)<br>- Use and limitations of scientific models<br>- Evaluating scientific claims and identifying pseudoscience<br>- Analyzing science in the media<br>- Ethical dilemmas and assessment frameworks for technology |
