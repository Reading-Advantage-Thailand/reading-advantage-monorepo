# Initial Concept

We are gong to create some vocabulary games that will be used in a next.js 15 environment, taking in a list of vocabulary with translations. The games will finish by awarding some XP for the player.

# Product Guide

## Vision
To develop a high-engagement "sandbox" of arcade-style vocabulary games designed for a Next.js 15 environment. These games serve as exciting alternatives to traditional study methods like flashcards, focusing on fast-paced recognition and typing proficiency.

## Target Users
*   **Language Learners (Self-study):** Individuals looking for gamified ways to reinforce their vocabulary.
*   **Classroom Students:** Students using the games as part of their assigned curriculum.
*   **Teachers:** Instructors who can project the games in front of a class for collective play and engagement.

## Core Game Modes
*   **Missile Command (Typing):** The primary focus. Players defend bases by typing the correct translations of incoming "missiles" representing vocabulary terms.
*   **Castle Defense:** A tower defense game where players collect vocabulary words to build and upgrade towers, defending their base against waves of enemies.
*   **Crossword Puzzles:** A classic interface where translations serve as clues to fill the grid.
*   **Tetris/Stacker Variant:** A hybrid mechanic where players must successfully answer multiple-choice questions to gain temporary control over falling blocks.
*   **Endless Runner:** A lane-switching or jumping game where the correct path is determined by identifying the right translation among several options.
*   **Rune Match (Match-3 RPG):** A tactical combat game where matching identical-looking vocabulary "runes" deals damage to monsters. Players must read the translations on the runes to find matches.
*   **Enchanted Library (Exploration):** A primary-school-friendly game where players control a student wizard collecting magic books in a haunted library. Features a mana-based scoring system, a shield mechanic with reflection physics, and progressive difficulty as ghostly spirits become faster.
*   **Potion Rush (Time Management):** A fast-paced game where players fulfill potion orders by collecting word "ingredients" from a conveyor belt. It features intelligent spawning (only needed words appear), a reputation-based health system, and dynamic difficulty scaling that reduces customer patience and increases belt speed over time.

## Progression and XP
The games will conclude by awarding XP to the player. The calculation will be based on:
*   **Accuracy:** A high percentage of correct answers leads to higher XP rewards.
*   **Speed:** Finishing games quickly or responding faster to prompts provides a "Time Bonus."

## Long-term Potential
*   Development of competitive and cooperative modes similar to Quizlet Live or Quizizz to enhance classroom dynamics.
